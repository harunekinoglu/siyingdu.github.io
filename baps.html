Instructional Design | Bilingual Assessment of Phonological Sensitivity (BAPS) Project

Front End Design


Bilingual Assessment of Phonological Sensitivity (BAPS) is a tool to evaluate a number of pre-literacy skills (together referred to as phonological sensitivity) in Spanish-English bilingual children between 3 and 6 years old (3 years 6 months - 5 years 11 months). The target of assessment is phonological knowledge/awareness/sensitivity. Finger grained abilities include rhyming, blending, segmenting, and sound matching. No tools yet exist that are designed to evaluate phonological sensitivity in Spanish-English bilinguals. Phonological sensitivity is heavily linked to reading development, and information about the development of pre-literacy skills, such as those included under the term phonological sensitivity, are urgently needed to better serve Spanish-English bilingual children.

The goal of BAPS is to address the need for a tool designed to evaluate phonological sensitivity in Spanish-English bilinguals.
